I am a cognitive developmental scientist with interdisciplinary training in developmental psychology, vision science, neuroscience, and quantitative methods. I study how infants and toddlers learn from everyday experiences such as social interactions and play, and how these experiences shape the cognitive and perceptual skills that support future learning.

Originally from California, I completed my PhD at the University of California, Davis with Dr. Lisa Oakes and later joined Cornell University as a postdoctoral researcher with Dr. Marianella Casasola.

Outside the lab, I enjoy watching anime, powerlifting, and spending time with my partner and our rescue pit bull, Chip.
